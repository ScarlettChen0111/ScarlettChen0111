INTERLINKED



Overview

Today, we live in a world where our existence is based more and more on the symbols we create. It seems that individualism has risen, but at the same time everyone is connected more closely than anytime in history due to the development of the internet. Rising individualism and complicated connections bring challenges, but it also opens up more opportunities for individuals to choose the lifestyles they want. As a result, I decided to incorporate the refinement that is inspired by Alexander McQueen’s skull series and combine the refining style with the notions of “connection” and “interlink” to make a collection of accessories that shows my appreciation for this new era.




Inspirations

Here are the two main inspirational sources for this jewelry project. The upper is my anatomy study drawing, and the bottom are jewelries by Alexander McQueen (pictures via Sak Fifth Avenue and Nordstrom official websites). Skull always reminds me of evolution, which is a topic I’ve been paying attention to. I’ve seen many skull jewelry, and Alexander McQueen’s skull collection is the most refined one among what I’ve seen. McQueen’s accessories are generally refined and exaggerated. Every single piece is a condensed magnificence. I admire the refinement. However, the overly decorative style is not for my taste. McQueen’s jewelry reminds me of an ancient European royal. Since I live in the 21st century and have a fast-paced lifestyle, I’m more inclined to modern sophistication. Compared to other skull jewelry from Amazon or those more affordable ones, the refined texture, size and materials of McQueen’s works make me realize that the quality and appearance of jewelry is largely determined by the maker's attention to details.

Printed Models



Inner Connection Of The Split Skull.



Material: Gold plated brass

Width: 0.92 inches

Height: 0.71 inches

Thickness: 0.78 inches

The splitted version expresses my reflection on the form of human life and our self consciousness in this cyber era. The split skull symbolizes opening up one’s mind and observing the essentiality of our existence. The wires in between the split pieces indicate that our brain works similarly to an electronic device. It is a bioprocessor. Our understanding of the world changes as technology develops. Humans are visual animals. Our body functions based on carbon-based tissues, cells, neurons, and reflex-arcs. Our so-called “rationality” is based on entities. As a result, humans can be seen as a kind of machine made of bioprocessors and programs, while machinery can be viewed as a new form of life.



Skulls Connected



Material: Gold plated brass, rhodium plated brass

Length: 2.5 inches

Width: 0.57 inches

Thickness: 0.74 inches

Skulls Connected shows my appreciation for this new century. The two skulls symbolize each individual of the collective. The wires connecting them symbolize that we are increasingly connected with each other. In the past, when two individuals were physically separated, their resources and connections could be barely shared with each other, which made their social circles small and limited opportunities to explore new lifestyles. Today, thanks to the development of the internet, people can make friends, find resources and new chances online. This is an overall positive change, so I’d like to create something tangible and wearable that incorporates the spirit of this new era.
