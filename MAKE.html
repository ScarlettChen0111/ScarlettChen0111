M A K E


Overview, Background And Inspiration

Overview

· "MAKE" is an experimental graphic commercial for the cosmetic surgery industry.
· It features a fictional plastic surgery agency, also named MAKE, known for professionalism, reliability, and access to advanced cosmetic surgery technology.
· The commercial consists of still images resembling a storybook.
· It utilizes cool-tone colors and biological visual elements like zygotes and ultrasound scans to suggest the potential for enhancing natural features.
· The narrative promotes the idea of "upgrading one's appearance to unlock a better version of oneself."
· It avoids exaggerated slogans, emphasizing the importance of clients making decisions based on their own will.
· The name "MAKE" reflects the belief that cosmetic surgery, done responsibly, is akin to creating artwork.

Academic project
Duration: 3 months
My role: Graphic Designer
Tools: Adobe Photoshop, Hand-made Illustrations




Background & Inspiration

· Human and ape skeletons/skulls evoke thoughts of evolution, particularly when juxtaposed.
· Comparing them reveals differences in walking posture and the refinement of the human skull compared to our ancestors.
· These disparities stem from adaptations to survival needs in varying environments.
· Apes and ancient humans evolved strong jaw bones and masseters to bite tough, rough foods.
· Consequently, their jaws became wider with maxillary and mandibular protrusion.
· In contrast, modern humans consume cooked, softer foods, eliminating the necessity for strong masseters and large jaws.
· Consequently, our jaws have evolved to smaller, narrower shapes, with the maxillary and mandibular structures tending inward compared to ancient humans.

· Modern human facial features symbolize civilization, resulting from the interaction between technological advancement and human brain evolution.
· Physical appearance changes can be viewed as a form of evolution.
· Efforts to enhance one's appearance are justifiable.
· Cosmetic surgery and individuals opting for it face judgmental attitudes, including accusations of fraudulence.
· Negative comments toward cosmetic surgery are unfair and hurtful.
· A sci-fi style commercial storybook for the cosmetic surgery industry aims to prompt reconsideration of the meaning of cosmetic surgery.
· The book advocates for using modern technology to upgrade one's appearance as justifiable and emblematic of civilization.

M A K E: Story Begin

The Cover


· The book cover features imagery centered around butterflies, symbolizing life, beauty, growth, and resurrection.
· Butterflies undergo a painful process of cocooning and emerging to achieve their complete form.
· The font used is Helvetica Regular, known for its clean-cut, smooth appearance with subtle, almost undetectable tails on some letters.
· Helvetica Regular strikes a balance between modernity and rationality, while also echoing the project's key themes of technology and beauty.




Page 1: Zygotes part 1


· The process of producing a newborn baby encompasses four stages: zygote, embryo, fetus, and finally, the baby is born.
· The depicted image illustrates the development of a zygote.
· Zygote is selected as the starting point of the story because, at this stage, everything remains unclear for the individual, including physical appearance.
· This choice symbolizes that as humans, we are entirely out of control of ourselves during this early stage of development.




Page 2: Zygotes part 2


The zygote is developing to a even more complicated form, and the zygote stage is almost done




Page 3: Ultrasound


· The individual has come to the fetus stage. The gift of life is almost about to be unpacked.




Page 4: Gift Of Life: Unpacked


· The box is a metaphor: life is a gift. It’s opened, and the features of the receiver(the new born baby) will be revealed. The letter A is the first letter in the alphabet and symbolizes that what’s in the box is the initial configuration. The letter A is from the word MAKE. The four letters “M””A””K””E” will appear separately in different pages of the whole work.




Page 5: Defective Gift


· This unattractive face is sketched by transforming my own selfie into an ugly look. This is the stage where everything is revealed. In terms of physical appearance, the individual has received a defective gift.
On the other hand, realizing one’s own defectiveness can be a turning point: someone has got an unattractive face from the god, and this person wants a better version of self.




Page 6: Scanning


· This image is the scanning process which takes place before the doctor making operation plans for the customer. The scanning will be 360-degree. The analysis for customer’s natural features such as bone structure, muscle thickness, and facial feature distribution will be accurate and precise.




Page 7: Eye Selection


· This stage involves the client selecting operation options.
· Options are generated based on scanning analysis.
· The first session focuses on the eyes.
· Five possible options for fixing the eyes are presented on the left-hand side of the image.
· The right-hand side shows a simulation of how the selected option will appear in real life.
· The dashed-line rectangle highlights the selection, with the letter "E" representing "eyes."




Page 8: Lip Selection


· The second session focuses on the lips.
· Four lip options are available.
· The simulation of the chosen lips is displayed on the right-hand side of the image.
· A letter "M" accompanies the selection.
· "M" stands for "Mark," implying that the client is highly inclined to finalize this choice.




Page 9: Contour Refinement


· The final session focuses on contour options for the side view of the face.
· Operations for side view enhancement may include rhinoplasty, chin implant, or bone shaving.
· Contour refinement is prioritized in the last session due to its typically higher cost and risk.
· In actual operations, contour adjustment is often the initial procedure, as the facial structure serves as the foundation for other enhancements.
· The selection is denoted by a cyan rose instead of a rectangle.
· The zoom-in view on the right highlights the chosen option, with pink dashed lines emphasizing slant angles forming the letter "K".




Page 10: The Closing Page


· The book concludes with a neon blue slogan in complete darkness, emphasizing the word "MAKE" in all caps, symbolizing both the operation and the agency's capability to enhance individuals.
· While the book ends, the narrative continues, encouraging readers to contemplate various outcomes.
· It doesn't showcase the final surgical result, leaving room for readers to envision potential changes in appearance, mentality, and life.
· Its purpose is to prompt potential clients to consider the possible scenarios post-operation, acknowledging that real-life outcomes may differ slightly from the planned result, despite the surgeon's skill.
