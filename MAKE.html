M A K E



Overview, Background And Inspiration

Overview

MAKE is an experimental graphic commercial designed for the cosmetic surgery industry. The commercial is designed for a fictional plastic surgery agency named MAKE. In my setting, MAKE is professional, reliable, and owns the latest cosmetic surgery technology. This graphic commercial is constituted by a series of still images and works as a small story book. The story is told by incorporating cool-tone colors that symbolize technology and biological visual elements of mankind such as zygote and ultrasound scanning of a baby, indicating the potential of upgrading our original body.

By showing the process of how a person is born and made beautiful later on, the story book promotes the idea of “upgrade one’s look and unlock a better version of oneself”. It has no exaggerated slogan, because MAKE believes that truly successful change can only be made when clients have enough mind space to think through their choice and make decisions 100% based on their own will. I name this fictional agency MAKE because from my perspective, a responsible and professional cosmetic surgery operation is very similar to making an artwork.




Background & Inspiration

Human and apes skeletons/skulls always remind me of evolution, especially when they are shown together. From the comparison, we can not only see the different walking positions, but also see how the human skull is more refined than our ancestors. These different features are determined by the survival needs under different circumstances. Apes and ancient humans were forced to evolve strong jaw bones and masseters to bite, since their foods were rough and hard to chew. It resulted in wider jaws, maxillary and mandibular protrusion. On the contrary, modern human has cooked, softer foods, which means strong masseters and big jaws are no longer needed. As a result, our jaws have devolved to smaller, narrower shapes. Our maxillary and mandibular tend to go inward compared to ancient humans.



By observing the differences between modern and ancient human facial features, I realize that in some way, modern human’s look symbolizes our civilization that happens due to the interplay between technology development and human brain evolution. As a result, the change of physical appearance can be seen as a kind of evolution as well.

In my opinion, it is totally justifiable if someone wants to make an effort to look better. However, cosmetic surgery and people who go through operations are still receiving judgmental responses today. I’ve even been told by some acquaintances that they thought doing plastic surgery was fraudulent. Such negative comments are undoubtedly unfair and hurtful to those who simply want themselves to look better. As a result, I came up with this sci-fi style commercial story book for the cosmetic surgery industry. The book is meant to guide my audiences to rethink about the meaning of cosmetic surgery: using modern technology to upgrade one’s look to a better version of him/herself is justifiable, and is a symbol of civilization.

M A K E: Story Begin

The Cover


This is the cover of the book. The center of the imagery is butterflies which not only symbolize life and beauty, but also growth and resurrection, because this creature has to go through a painful process of wrapping itself in a cocoon and breaking the cocoon later on in order to achieve its complete form.
The font is Helvetica Regular. It’s clean-cut, smooth and square-looking, while some letters have low-key, almost undetectable tails. In result, Helvetica Regular successfully balances its modern and rational with a hint of softness, echoing with the two key words of the project: technology and beauty.




Page 1: Zygotes part 1


Producing a new-born baby goes through 4 stages: zygote—embryo—fetus—baby(born). This image shows the development of a zygote.
I chose zygote to start the story because at this stage, everything is unclear for the person, including physical appearance. It indicates that as a human, we are totally out of control of ourselves at this stage.




Page 2: Zygotes part 2


In this image, the zygote is developing to a even more complicated form, and the zygote stage is almost done




Page 3: Ultrasound


The individual has come to the fetus stage. The gift of life is almost about to be unpacked.




Page 4: Gift Of Life: Unpacked


The box is a metaphor: life is a gift. It’s opened, and the features of the receiver(the new born baby) will be revealed. The letter A is the first letter in the alphabet and symbolizes that what’s in the box is the initial configuration. The letter A is from the word MAKE. The four letters “M””A””K””E” will appear separately in different pages of the whole work.




Page 5: Defective Gift


This unattractive face is sketched by transforming my own selfie into an ugly look. This is the stage where everything is revealed. In terms of physical appearance, the individual has received a defective gift.
On the other hand, realizing one’s own defectiveness can be a turning point: someone has got an unattractive face from the god, and this person wants a better version of self.




Page 6: Scanning


This image is the scanning process which takes place before the doctor making operation plans for the customer. The scanning will be 360-degree. The analysis for customer’s natural features such as bone structure, muscle thickness, and facial feature distribution will be accurate and precise.




Page 7: Eye Selection


This is the stage where the client chooses operation options. The options are generated based on the scanning analysis. The first session is the eyes. The left hand side of the image is the five possible options for fixing the eyes, and the right hand side is the simulation of how the selection will look like in real life. The dashed-line rectangle encircles the selection. The letter “E” refers to the word “eyes”.




Page 8: Lip Selection


The second session is the lips. There are four options for doing the lips, and the right hand side of the image is showing the simulation of the selected lips. The letter M appears next to the selection. “M” refers to the word “Mark”, which indicates that the client is very likely to make this selection her final choice.




Page 9: Contour Refinement


The final session is the contour options, which specifies the side view of the face. Operations for side view may involve rhinoplasty, chin implant or bone shaving. The contour refinement is arranged in the last session because this is usually the most expensive and riskiest part. However, when it comes to real operation, if someone decides to fix the contour, it is always the first operation as facial structure is the base for other features. The selection is marked by a cyan rose instead of a rectangle. The right hand side is the zoom-in view of the selected option. The pink dashed lines emphasize the slant angles for the chosen view and compose the letter “K”.




Page 10: The Closing Page


The book is ended by emphasizing the slogan in a complete darkness using neon blue with an outer glow. The word “make” is written using all capital letters. It not only means the operation, but also echoes with the agency’s name MAKE, as well as emphasizes the fact that the agency is capable of making customers better versions of themselves.

Although the book is finished, the story is not. Different people may have different thoughts after reading this short, commercial story book. The book doesn’t have a showcase for the final operation result. The final look, potential change of the person’s mentality and life are for readers to imagine. It is meant to guide potential clients to think about potential scenarios of their life after operation. In fact, in real life, no matter how precisely the operation plan is, the final outcome will not be exactly the same as planned. Highly skilled surgeons will make the look as natural and closely to the plan as possible, but still, they won’t be the same.
