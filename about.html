I'm Scarlett Chen, an interdisciplinary designer focusing on 3D modeling, video, and visual communication. I grew up in the most inclusive city in China--Guangzhou, which is next to Shenzhen and Hong Kong. I have lived in the US for 7 years. My experience of living and studying in the US, along with my experience in my hometown, have shaped my open-minded, rational and independent personality. From my perspective, in most cases, there's no right or wrong, but different needs, interests, and fears of different people.

I always try to get along with people around me, and help them live their best life while working on my own. However, I tend not to get too attached to a specific place, or a group, which means I don't define my identity based on specific details e.g. where I from, or where I graduate, etc. It also means that no matter how long I've been in a certain place or community, I always leave myself space to think about the big picture from a more objective perspective.

To me, an individual's identity is based on what the person believes, what the person is willing to do for that belief. As a result, identity is not static, it's open to transformation.
