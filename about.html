Tell me a little bit about your self?

I'm Scarlett Chen, an interdisciplinary designer focusing on 3D modeling, video, and visual communication. I grew up in the most inclusive city in China--Guangzhou, which is next to Shenzhen and Hong Kong. I have lived in the US for 7 years. My experience of living and studying in the US, along with my experience in my hometown, have shaped my open-minded, rational and independent personality. From my perspective, in most cases, there's no right or wrong, but different needs, interests, and fears of different people.

I always try to get along with people around me, and help them live their best life while working on my own. However, I tend not to get too immersive to a specific place, or a group, which means I don't define my identity based on specific details e.g. where I from, or where I graduate, etc. It also means that no matter how long I've been in a certain place or community, I always leave myself space to think about the big picture from a more objective perspective.

To me, an individual's identity is based on what the person believes, what the person is willing to do for that belief. As a result, identity is not static, it's open to transformation.

What are your strong points and weak points?

My biggest strong point is:

I'm both goal-oriented and detail-oriented.
I always ask two questions before I start working: (1) What does my instructor/client expect for the final deliverable(even a few adjectives work if it's not specific). (2) What possible significance/meaning(s) the project can have.
Even if I'm going to research different directions, knowing where I'm going helps a lot. I use these two questions to set tangible goals and avoid waste of time.

When I start working, I care a lot for details. From font size to subtle color difference, or line thickness, details determine whether a project will look professional and expensive or not.

My biggest weak point is:

I can be overly obsessive about whether a project is successful and looks perfect or not. Sometimes I can be too obsessive about certain design details, which sometimes slows my working pace. However, based on my past experiences, I was always able to get things done by deadline in the end. I will keep working on better managing my time and balance perfection and productivity.
